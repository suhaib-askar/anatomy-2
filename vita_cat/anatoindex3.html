Vitamin A

Supports: Eye health,helps form and maintain healthy teeth, skeletal and soft tissue, mucus membranes, and skin

Foods: Carrots, Sweet Potato, Liver, Kale

Deficiency Symptoms: Dry eyes, dry skin, frequent infections

Vitamin B1 (Thiamine)

Supports: Metabolizing energy, cellular growth and function

Foods: Beans, Sunflower Seeds, Rice

Deficiency Symptoms: Confusion, Lack of appetite, difficulty balancing

Vitamin B2 (Riboflavin)

Supports: Growth of body, red blood cell production

Foods: Dairy, Eggs, Spinach, Liver

Deficiency Symptoms: Irritated eyes, itchy nose and throat

Foods: Milk, Eggs, Meat Rice

Supports: Helps release energy from the food you eat

Foods: Fish, Egg, Meat, Potatoes

Deficiency Symptoms: Skin inflammation

Vitamin B9 (Folic Acid)

Supports: Helps synthesze DNA, production of growth hormones

Foods: Spinach, Asparagus, Broccoli, Beans

Deficiency Symptoms: Fatigue, Anemia

Vitamin B12 (Cobalamin)

Supports: Helps with the process to create blood cells and regulate brain function

Foods: Milk, Fish, Beef, Lamb, Yogurt

Deficiency Symptoms: Memory issues, fatigue, anemia

Vitamin C (Asorbic Acid)

Supports: Strengthens blood vessels, skin elasicity, anti-oxidant, iron absorbtion

Foods: Capsicum (peppers), Papaya, Strawberries, Tomatoes

Deficiency Symptoms: Bleeding gums, Fatigue

Vitamin D (Cholecalciferol)

Function: Supports and strengthens healthy bones

Food: Milk, Eggs, Mushrooms, Fish

Deficiency Symptoms: Weakened bones, unhealthy teeth

Vitamin E (Alpha-Tocopherol)

Supports: Protects from free radicals, improves blood circulation

Foods: Carrots, Nuts, Olives, Raspberries, Capsicum

Deficiency Symptoms: Muscle weakness, fertility issues

Vitamin K (Phytonadione)

Supports: Helps your blood coagulate

Foods: Broccoli, Spinach, Brussel, Sprouts, Kale

Deficiency Symptoms: Lightheadedness, dizziness, exhaustion
